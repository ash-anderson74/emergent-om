# Core Domains

EmergentOS is composed of a small set of **core domains**, each representing a distinct dimension of adaptation within a complex socio-technical system.

Domains are not organizational units, layers, or phases.  
They describe **where change, learning, and alignment occur**, regardless of structure.

Together, they define the minimum set of concerns required for sustained adaptability.

---

| Domain                   | Primary Focus                                   | Guiding Question                              | Primary Levers                                                                 |
| ------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Direction & Purpose      | Strategic intent and shared outcomes             | Why are we adapting?                          | Mission clarity, adaptive strategy, outcome framing, sensing mechanisms         |
| Leadership & Culture     | Behavioural and social system conditions         | How do we lead and learn together?            | Decision rights, leadership models, trust, learning culture, psychological safety |
| Product & Value Creation | Discovery, delivery, and value evolution         | How does value emerge over time?              | Product operating model, empowered teams, discovery practices, outcome signals  |
| Flow & Enablement        | System capacity for safe and responsive change   | How does work move and adapt safely?          | Technical excellence, architecture, automation, enabling infrastructure, flow constraints |
| Learning & Sensing       | Feedback and systemic understanding              | How do we know what to change next?           | Reflection, experimentation, telemetry, feedback loops, sensing networks        |

---

## Domain Relationships

The core domains are **interdependent and non-hierarchical**.

No domain is primary, and no domain operates in isolation.  
Changes in one domain inevitably affect the others.

Rather than forming a linear sequence, the domains operate as a **continuous adaptive loop**.

## Systemic Interaction

Across the system:

- Direction establishes intent and bounds exploration
- Leadership shapes the conditions under which adaptation occurs
- Product teams explore and validate value
- Flow enables rapid, safe feedback
- Learning and sensing inform future direction and leadership choices

Breakdown in any single domain degrades the effectiveness of the whole.

---

## Cross-Domain Invariants

EmergentOS applies a consistent set of invariants across all domains:

- Empowerment over control
- Outcomes over outputs
- Learning over certainty
- Trust over compliance
- Flow over friction
- Systems thinking over local optimization

These invariants guide interpretation and decision-making within every domain rather than acting as independent principles.
